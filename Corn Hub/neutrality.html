Corn Hub - Net Neutrality

The big debate

Sooo... What is "Net Neutrality"?

In a nutshell, Net Neutrality is an agreement between you and your Internet Service Provider (ISP) saying it is not allowed to artificially slow down or block specific sites or content based on what the ISP determines should be fast, slow, or blocked, but instead to serve all things equally. Net Neutrality means that your Internet Service Provider will treat sites such as 'Google' and 'Whatever this site is' equally when you are using them and give you the same speed, wether you be streaming video, playing an online game, or reading a forum.

Recently, Ajit Pai, chairman of the Federal Communications Commision, is focused on (as he said it): “taking a weed whacker” to the Net Neutrality framework that was passed by the FCC in 2015 under President Obama. Ajit has already started whacking away. In May, Pai introduced a new proposal that would undo the Obama-era net neutrality rules, which the current Republican-led FCC could vote on by the end of 2017.

This has caused much uproar in the community of the internet, and many sites have even started petitions to keep the Net Neutrality rules.
